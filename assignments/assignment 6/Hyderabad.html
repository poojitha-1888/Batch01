Hyderabad

Hyderabad, the capital of Telangana, is a vibrant metropolis on the Deccan Plateau known as the "City of Pearls" and a major IT hub. Founded in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, it blends rich Islamic and Hindu history—exemplified by the iconic Charminar—with modern development. Renowned for its unique Hyderabadi cuisine, including Biryani and Haleem, the city offers a diverse culture, bustling markets, and landmarks like the Golconda Fort.

Hyderabad is known as the City of Pearls, as it had once flourished as a global center for trade of rare diamonds, emeralds as well as natural pearls. Mecca Masjid, Charminar, Golconda fort, QutubShahi tombs, Paigah tombs are some of the historic structures.
